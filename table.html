GHANAIAN DAY NAMES

CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE OF GHANAIAN DAY NAMES

Ghanaian day names are an integral part of the country's cultural identity and heritage. In Ghana, traditional day names are assigned to individuals based on the day of the week they were born. These names are deeply rooted in Akan culture and are believed to have a significant influence on a person's character and destiny. Each day of the week is associated with a specific name, and these names are used in addition to one's given name. For example, "Kwame" is given to individuals born on a Saturday, while "Akosua" is given to those born on a Sunday.
